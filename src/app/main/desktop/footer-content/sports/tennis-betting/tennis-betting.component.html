History of Tennis

The history of tennis is quite long and filled with thrilling details. It is believed that the game we today call tennis emerged in Northern France during the 12th century when the players struck the ball with the palm of their hand rather than a racket. A few called it jeu de paume which translates to game of the palm in english.

People started utilizing rackets somewhere during the 16th century, and the game came to be known as tennis, which emerged from the French tenez, meaning hold.

The origination of the newest game dates back to the middle of the 19th century. People credited for launching modern tennis are 2 sportsmen called Augurio Pereira and Harry Gem. in 1874, British army officer Walter Clopton Wingfield altered the game and substantially donated to its fame. This was also when the very first tennis tournament occurred in Birmingham.

Few years later in 1877, the Croquet Club and All England Lawn Tennis held the very first Championship at Wimbledon. Simultaneously, the game of tennis went across the Atlantic and reached the US, where in 1880 the first American National Championship occurred.

A year later, the first US Open was held in Rhode Island, Newport. The Australian Open and French Open originated in 1905 and 1891, respectively. The first Davis Cup, which only included a battle among Great Britain and the United States, started in 1900 and was subsequently expanded to include other nations.

Tennis was part of the initial Olympic Games in Athens in 1896, but it was taken out from the programme in 1924. The sport made a comeback to the Games in Seoul in 1988.

For a major part of tennis history, this was an amateur sport. Professionals existed, but they were not permitted to battle in leading tournaments. This changed up in 1968 when Grand Slams consented to host pros, marking the start of the Open Era.
